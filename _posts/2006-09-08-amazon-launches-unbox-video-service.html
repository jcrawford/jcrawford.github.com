Amazon launched its own video download service on Thursday called Unbox.  They are offering you 1 free TV Show download ($1.99 value) so that you will try thier service.  I quickly ran over to download a TV Show only to find that in order to download a show you need to use the Unbox Video Player.  I half expected this, however what i did not expect is a Windows ONLY software.  I figured someone as big as Amazon would have a cross platform application that would run on ANY operating system.  I was wrong, so i turned on my computer that is running Vista Pre-RC1, downloaded the installtion files and started the process.  Wow wouldn't you know it the application will not install.  It requires XP SP 2 or Windows 2003.  Already i am not a fan of Unbox, although i have not been able to test the quality of the video's they are claiming them to be DVD quality.  Every purchase also comes with 2 files, one for you to watch on the computer/tv and the other for portable devices.  I am guessing they mean Windows based portable devices so all of you iPod owners are out of luck.

Unbox will offer both TV Shows and Movies for sale.  You can buy a movie for $7.99-$14.99 and TV Shows are $1.99.  Unbox is taking it one step furthur and offering rentals.  You can pay $3.99 for a rental which will disable itself 24 hours after you watch it.  I think this is a good idea since $3.99 is the typical rental price and the costs for making the DVD packaging would offset the cost of the movies here.

I really do hope Apple starts the Movie service everyone is talking about, and i hope that they are cross platform so that everyone can enjoy the service that Apple will provide.  I cannot express enough how unimpressed i am with Amazon today.
